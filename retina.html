SYMPTOMS OF RETINAL DISORDERS

Retinal disorders are associated with vision loss and quality of vision. Depending on the type of the disorder, symptoms can be gradual or sudden. Ageing is one of the most common causes. Consider regular eye check up for timely diagnosis

  • Floaters accompanied by eye flashes
  • Sudden onset of blurred vision
  • Shadows or blind spots in the field of vision
  • Increase in number of floaters

FAQ
How to prevent retinal detachment?

You can lower risks of retinal detachment by opting for regular eye care, using safety goggles while playing sports or doing something risky and by getting prompt treatment from a reliable eye doctor.

How long does it take to recover from retina eye surgery?

The recovery time depends on a variety of factors, including the individual's overall health, the type of surgery performed, and how they approach the post-surgical period.
The recovery time for pneumatic retinopexy is approximately three weeks while the recovery time for scleral buckling is approximately two to four weeks. The recovery time for vitrectomy is approximately four to six weeks.

does everyone with high numbers need a retina check-up?

Even if you have never worn glasses, a regular retina eye exam is essential. Even if you have had twenty-twenty vision your entire life, your eyes will not be the same once you reach the age of 50. The reason for this is simple: just as your physical health declines with age, so does your vision.
